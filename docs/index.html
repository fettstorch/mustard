Hello Mustard!

Add your opinion to any webpage — right-click anywhere on this page and leave your first published mustard for your friends to see!

What is Mustard?

Mustard lets you annotate any webpage with notes — like adding your "mustard" (from the German saying "seinen Senf dazu geben"). Save notes locally or publish them to your Bluesky followers.

Ideas for your Mustard

  • Share your thoughts on something you just read
  • Provide additional context to a blog post
  • Fact-check a news article
  • Just slap a funny gif on something

How to use

  1. Right-click on any element on a webpage and select "Add Mustard"
  2. Write your note (up to 300 characters)
  3. Click the floppy disk to save locally (no account needed) or the blue arrow to publish (requires Bluesky login)

Tips

  • Drag notes around if they're covering something you want to see
  • Toggle notes off via the extension popup — click the Mustard bottle icon in your toolbar
  • Markdown supported — use **bold**, *italic*, [links](url), and more
  • Add images & gifs by pasting an image link into your note
  • Delete your own notes by clicking the bin icon

Do not publish sensitive information

Never include passwords, API keys, personal addresses, or other private data in published notes. Published notes are stored on our server and visible to anyone who follows you. Treat them like public posts.

Heads up

  • Content script on all pages: Mustard injects a content script on every page you visit to display notes. It does not read or collect any page data — it only checks if there are notes for the current URL.
  • Local notes are truly local: Notes saved locally never leave your browser. They're stored in Chrome's extension storage.
  • Early stage: Mustard is in early development. Expect rough edges, and please report any bugs!
